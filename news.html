My 2023 1st round Mock Draft

Start of the draft

  1. Connor Bedard, Chicago Blackhawks

    2. I think this is a lock. The Blackhawks get the best prospect since Mcdavid.

  2. Adam Fantilli, Anaheim Ducks

    3. I'm 6/10 on a confidence scale that Anaheim selects Fantilli here. He had a home-run season in the NCAA.

  3. Leo Carlsson, Columbus Blue Jackets

    4. Carlsson fits everything Jarmo looks for in the draft. A big center with no. 1 potential? Sign me up.

  4. Will Smith, San Jose Sharks

    5. I tried looking at San Jose's track record and I found they like to pick players with a ton of skill. Smith has plenty of it.

  5. Ryan Leonard, Montreal Canadiens

    6. This one was between Reinbacher and Leonard. Two players that play a physical and skilled hockey style. I went with Leonard because I believe he's the better player.

  6. Dalibor Dvorsky, Arizona Coyotes

    7. I'm a firm believer the Coyotes like Dvorsky's style of play. He is dominant at 5v5, has some skill, but lacks footpacing. That is exactly the type of player I expect Arizona to draft.

  7. David Reinbacher, Philadelphia Flyers

    8. Reinbacher is the #1 consensus for defensemen at the moment and Philly is in full rebuild mode. He is a great first step at rebuilding their blue-line

  8. Matvei Michkov, Washington Capitals

    9. The Capitals aren't scared of drafting Russian players and Michkov is the second best talent in the draft class. I don't think he falls further than this.

  9. Zach Benson, Detroit Red Wings

    10. Yzerman plans on drafting the best player available and I think that means Benson or Perreault here. I have them drafting Benson due to his high compete level.

  10. Tom Willander, St. Louis Blues

    11. St. Louis goes with the biggest riser and one of the smartest players in the draft.

  11. Nate Danielson, Vancouver Canucks

    12. Danielson bring intangibles and fits the ideal center mold Vancouver has been looking for behind Pettersson.

  12. Gabe Perreault, Arizona Coyotes

    13. Arizona needs defensemen, but I think they get an extraordinary skilled forward in Perreault as BPA.

  13. Dmitri Simashev, Buffalo Sabres

    14. Simashev is exactly what Buffalo needs, a big smooth skating defender. They have drafted all their small skilled players already. Time to add size and skating around them.

  14. Daniil But, Pittsburgh Penguins

    15. This could've gone a couple of ways. The big Russian is a great piece to start the new chapter for the Penguins. He brings size, skill, and speed.

  15. Axel Sandin-Pelikka, Nashville Predators

    16. The Predators are in dire need of high-end defensive prospects. I think Axel is one of the best defenders on the board, so they choose him.

  16. Colby Barlow, Calgary Flames

    17. With so many players leaving Calgary, it's time for a culture change with Barlow. Barlow is a safer player with great intangibles. I think he is the perfect fit for the Flames.

  17. Samuel Honzek, Detroit Red Wings

    18. Honzek said himself that he thought his interview with the Red Wings was the best one. He is exactly what the Wings are looking for: size, speed, and skill.

  18. Matthew Wood, Winnipeg Jets

    19. Wood drops a bit because of his skating, but the Jets are not afraid to draft questionable skaters. Wood is their guy with size and high level playmaking abilities.

  19. Brayden Yager, Chicago Blackhawks

    20. Ideally the Hawks draft bigger players to go along with their small skilled forward prospects, but Yager fits their type to a tee.

  20. Oliver Moore, Seattle Kraken

    21. Moore likely doesn't fall this far, but the Kraken are happy to take him here. An elite skater that adds to an already explosive team.

  21. David Edstrom, Minnesota Wild

    22. Joel Eriksson Ek 2.0, enough said

  22. Quentin Musty, Philadelphia Flyers

    23. Musty brings such a high level playmaking ability, it is tough for me to see too many teams pass on him. The Flyers nab themselves an offensive dynamo.

  23. Oliver Bonk, New York Rangers

    24. The Rangers take a safer and bigger option to surround Adam Fox on the blueline. Bonk is as safe as you can get at this point.

  24. Otto Stenberg, Nashville Predators

    25. The Predators go with another Swede and add-on to the skill they have been drafting the past couple years.

  25. Oskar-Fisker Molgard, St. Louis Blues

    26. Maybe the biggest surprise so far. Oskar is the most NHL ready player left that I can see the Blues targeting.

  26. Calum Ritchie, San Jose Sharks

    27. The Sharks go with another skill player down the middle. A team that definitely needs a strong center pipeline.

  27. Tanner Molendyk, Colorado Avalanche

    28. It was between Tanner and Mikhail, but I don't think the Aves take a risk on a Russian, so they go with the best skating defender left.

  28. Eduard Sale, Toronto Maple Leafs

    29. Sale has the size and skill of a good NHL player. The leafs aren't afraid of swinging for the fences and I think they do that with Sale.

  29. Bradly Nadeau, St. Louis Blues

    30. After drafting two safer and more projectible players, I think the Blues go with a high-end skill player with upside. Nadeau brings that to the table.

  30. Charlie Stramel, Carolina Hurricanes

    31. Carolina goes after the gritty energy player in Stramel that can elevate their team in the playoffs.

  31. Ethan Gauthier, Montreal Canadiens

    32. The Canadiens go for a French winger that brings all the attributes they look for in players: grit, character, and skill.

  32. Carson Rehkopf, Vegas Golden Knights

    33. A toolsy player with size is the Golden Knights MO. They continue with that here.
