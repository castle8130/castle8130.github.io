The 2023 entry level draft

Summary of the draft

The 2023 draft is a fun group of players to watch. At the top of the draft you have Connor Bedard, Adam Fantilli, Matvei Michkov, Leo Carlsson, and Will Smith. Bedard is a WJC-20 record setting individual that is being put in a category with Mcavid and Crosby by many. Fantilli has already won the Hobey Baker award, a WJC-20 gold medal, and a WC gold medal. Michkov has produced at the highest point per game rate by a KHL u-18 draft elgible ever. The two others mentioned are high profile cases too. Since all these players are so highly touted, this draft has been getting a ton of hype by fans and media. I am not quite there with this draft. The top 5 or so players are all quite exceptional and are better than most drafts, but the depth of the draft is pretty average to below average. I have heard from a few sources (that talk to scouts) that the draft isn't anything special outside the top 5. The forward depth in this draft is a bit stronger than usual due to the exceptional top 5. The defensive class is a bit weaker with no true high-end defenders. The highest ranked defensemen on my list and most consensus lists is David Reinbacher and many people fear he doesn't have true #1 potential. Some see him as a second pairing defenseman and some see him as a #2. I'm more optimistic about Reinbacher and see him as a #2, but after him, the other top defenders aren't too promising for their draft position.
